Meet our animals

Did you know that the Axolotl, a unique species of salamander native to Mexico, has the extraordinary ability to regenerate its limbs, spinal cord, heart, and even parts of its brain? Unlike most other animals, the Axolotl can regrow entire body parts throughout its life, making it a fascinating subject of scientific research in the field of tissue regeneration. This incredible regenerative power has earned the Axolotl the nickname "the walking fish of hope" and makes it a truly remarkable amphibian.

Amphibians

Did you know that some parrot species have the remarkable ability to mimic human speech and other sounds with astonishing accuracy? Among the most famous avian linguists is the African Grey Parrot, known for its exceptional vocal skills and intelligence. These clever birds can learn to imitate various words, phrases, and even mimic the tones and cadences of human conversation. With their ability to mimic sounds, parrots have delighted and amazed humans for centuries, making them captivating companions and entertaining communicators in the avian world.

Birds

The adorable and iconic koala, known for its fluffy appearance and gentle demeanor, holds a fascinating secret: it has an incredibly specialized diet. These cuddly marsupials rely almost exclusively on eucalyptus leaves for sustenance, which not only provides their nutrition but also influences their unique charm.
